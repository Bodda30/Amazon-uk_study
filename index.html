AFN FBA CSBA MFN

1- AFN: Amazon Fulfillment Network

Sold: Fulfilled by Amazon (Prime covered)

Actions and Options:

  • Full refund: If the customer is unsatisfied with the product.
  • Replacement: If the product is defective.
  • Repair: When applicable, such as for electronics.
  • Discounts: For future purchases as a goodwill gesture.

2- FBA: Fulfilled by Amazon

Sold: Sold by seller, fulfilled and delivered by Amazon (Prime covered)

Actions and Options:

  • Full refund: If the product is not as described.

3- CSBA: Customer Service by Amazon

Sold: Fulfilled by seller, customer service and delivery by Amazon (Prime covered)

Actions and Options:

  • Full refund: If the product does not meet customer expectations.

4- MFN: Marketplace Fulfillment Network

Sold: Sold, fulfilled, delivered, and customer service by the seller. Amazon is just a platform.

Actions and Options:

  • Contact seller via email: Customers can reach out for inquiries.
  • Raise an A-Z claim: If the seller does not respond in 48 hours.

Seller has 2 days to respond. The response will appear in the message center, and the customer can reply.

If the seller doesn’t respond in 48 hours, an A-Z claim can be raised. Wait until EDD+3 days pass before raising the claim.

A-Z claims team will take over the case within 7 days.

Max Periods:

  • A-Z claim: 6 months
  • Appeal if rejected: 30 days

Empathy and Sympathy Examples

For Customer Service Agents:

  • Empathy: "I understand how frustrating it can be when an order doesn’t arrive on time. Let’s see what we can do to resolve this for you."
  • Sympathy: "I’m really sorry to hear that the product didn’t meet your expectations. Your satisfaction is important to us, and I’d like to help make this right."

How to Succeed in Amazon UK Customer Service

A to Z Information for Success:

  • A: Active listening: Always listen to the customer’s concerns without interruption.
  • B: Be patient: Customers may be upset; patience can help calm the situation.
  • C: Clear communication: Speak clearly and avoid jargon that the customer may not understand.
  • D: Demonstrate empathy: Show that you care about the customer’s feelings and situation.
  • E: Efficient problem-solving: Quickly assess issues and provide solutions.
  • F: Follow-up: Ensure that the customer is satisfied with the solution provided.
  • G: Grateful attitude: Thank customers for their patience and understanding.
  • H: Handling complaints: View complaints as opportunities to improve service.
  • I: Information accuracy: Provide accurate information and solutions.
  • J: Justify solutions: Explain the reasoning behind your suggestions.
  • K: Knowledgeable: Stay informed about Amazon policies and procedures.
  • L: Listen: Pay attention to both verbal and non-verbal cues.
  • M: Manage stress: Stay calm, even during challenging interactions.
  • N: Non-defensive: Take customer feedback constructively.
  • O: Offer alternatives: If a solution isn’t available, provide alternatives.
  • P: Personalize service: Use the customer's name and personalize interactions.
  • Q: Quick response: Aim to resolve issues in a timely manner.
  • R: Respect: Treat every customer with respect and kindness.
  • S: Stay calm: Keep your composure, even if the customer is upset.
  • T: Tone of voice: Maintain a friendly and approachable tone.
  • U: Understand policies: Familiarize yourself with Amazon’s return and refund policies.
  • V: Validate feelings: Let customers know their feelings are valid.
  • W: Willingness to help: Show enthusiasm in helping the customer.
  • X: eXpectations: Manage customer expectations realistically.
  • Y: You matter: Make customers feel valued and important.
  • Z: Zeal for service: Approach every interaction with passion and dedication.
