Order Types

Click on the order types to see more details:

AFN: (Sold and fulfilled by Amazon)

• Refund
• Replacement
• Repair
• Discount

FBA: (Fulfilled by Amazon, sold by third party seller)

• Refund only

CSBA: (Customer service by Amazon)

- Sold and fulfilled by third party seller
• Refund only
• Contact seller to confirm the return
• Contact seller in case of delay
• Contact seller for product troubleshooting

MFN: (Market place Fulfilment Network)

- Sold and fulfilled by third party seller
• Warranty by the seller
• Shipping fees set by the seller
• The only type of order which is not covered by Prime
Note:
➢ Whatever the customer requests, advise to contact the seller
➢ MFN covered by A-Z claim for 6 months
➢ To be able to raise the claim on the system:
✓ 48 hours need to pass after contacting the seller
✓ EDD + 3 days
✓ If the item is defective, RMA needs to be created and 48 hours passed.
➢ SLA for A-Z: (7 days/week) and could take up to a month.

Warehouse Deals:

(AFN items but used/missing accessories)
• Refund
• Discount

Global Store:

(Amazon sites)
• Refund on return only
Note:
➢ If the item is less than 30 GBP, we offer a returnless refund and advise the customer to keep, donate, or dispose of it.
➢ The amount of the return postage shouldn’t exceed 80% of the item total amount.
➢ If the customer confirms that the return postage will be more than the item amount, ask for proof and contact DART.
➢ If the customer asks for import fees refund, advise that it gets calculated automatically by the system and if the customer is eligible for a refund, it will be issued within 180 days.
➢ If the carrier contacts the customer to ask for extra money to deliver, advise the customer not to pay anything extra and that the carrier should contact Amazon to sort this out.

Try Before You Buy:

(Amazon Wardrobe)
• For Prime members only
• Ship up to 6 items at a time to try on free at home for 7 days
• Customer charged only for the items they select to keep in their orders (Keep or Return Items button)
• Items will arrive 1-2 business days after they place the order
• The try period starts after EDD + 3 days or after the last bag on the order is scanned as delivered.

  • Sympathy: "I’m really sorry to hear that you are experiencing this issue."
  • Empathy: "It sounds like you’ve been through a lot. Let’s see how we can help."

A to Z Information for Success

  • A: Attentive: Listen carefully to the customer’s needs.
  • B: Be patient: Stay calm, especially with difficult customers.
  • C: Communicate clearly: Use simple language.
  • D: Document interactions: Keep records of customer interactions.
  • E: Empathize: Show understanding for the customer’s situation.
  • F: Follow up: Ensure the issue is resolved satisfactorily.
  • G: Goal-oriented: Focus on resolving customer issues efficiently.
  • H: Honest: Be truthful about what you can offer.
  • I: Initiative: Take the lead in resolving customer problems.
  • J: Join the conversation: Engage actively with the customer.
  • K: Knowledge: Keep up to date with product knowledge and company policies.
  • L: Listen: Pay attention to what the customer is saying.
  • M: Manage time: Handle calls and inquiries efficiently without rushing.
  • N: Negotiate: Find common ground with the customer to resolve issues.
  • O: Offer solutions: Provide options that fit the customer's needs.
  • P: Positive Attitude: Maintain a positive demeanor even under pressure.
  • Q: Quality assurance: Ensure consistent and high-quality service.
  • R: Responsiveness: Respond to inquiries quickly and effectively.
  • S: Solutions-oriented: Focus on finding solutions rather than dwelling on problems.
  • T: Training: Regularly participate in training to enhance skills.
  • U: Uniqueness: Tailor your approach to each customer's needs.
  • V: Vigilance: Stay alert to customer cues and feedback.
  • W: Willingness to learn: Seek feedback to improve your skills.
  • X: eXchange: Encourage customers to share their experiences and suggestions.
  • Y: Yearn for improvement: Always look for ways to enhance your service.
  • Z: Zeal for knowledge: Stay curious and continuously learn.

Never Do List

  • 1. Never Ignore the Customer: Always acknowledge the customer's presence and their issues promptly.
  • 2. Never Be Rude or Disrespectful: Maintain a polite and respectful tone, regardless of the customer's attitude.
  • 3. Never Interrupt the Customer: Let the customer finish speaking before you respond.
  • 4. Never Blame the Customer: Avoid placing blame on the customer for the problem they are experiencing.
  • 5. Never Make Promises You Can't Keep: Only commit to actions you can deliver on to maintain trust.
  • 6. Never Overlook Follow-ups: Ensure to follow up on any commitments made to the customer.
  • 7. Never Assume You Understand: Clarify and ask questions if you're unsure about the customer's issue.
  • 8. Never Use Jargon: Avoid technical terms that the customer might not understand.
