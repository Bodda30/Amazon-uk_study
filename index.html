1- AFN: Amazon Fulfillment Network

Sold: Fulfilled by Amazon (Prime covered)

Actions and Options:

  • Full refund: If the customer is unsatisfied with the product.
  • Replacement: If the product is defective.
  • Repair: When applicable, such as for electronics.
  • Discounts: For future purchases as a goodwill gesture.

2- FBA: Fulfilled by Amazon

Sold: Sold by seller, fulfilled and delivered by Amazon (Prime covered)

Actions and Options:

  • Full refund: If the product is not as described.

3- CSBA: Customer Service by Amazon

Sold: Fulfilled by seller, customer service and delivery by Amazon (Prime covered)

Actions and Options:

  • Full refund: If the product does not meet customer expectations.

Amazon CTS

Never Do List

  • Never Ignore the Customer: Always acknowledge the customer's presence and their issues promptly.
  • Never Be Rude or Disrespectful: Maintain a polite and respectful tone, regardless of the customer's attitude.
  • Never Interrupt the Customer: Let the customer finish speaking before you respond.
  • Never Blame the Customer: Avoid placing blame on the customer for the problem they are experiencing.
  • Never Make Promises You Can't Keep: Only commit to actions you can deliver on to maintain trust.
  • Never Use Negative Language: Avoid phrases like “you can’t,” “I won’t,” “it’s not possible.” Instead, focus on what you can do.
  • Never Argue with the Customer: Stay calm and professional, even if the customer is angry or unreasonable.
  • Never Give Incomplete Information: Provide clear, complete, and accurate information to the customer.
  • Never Transfer Without Explanation: Always explain why a transfer is necessary and ensure the customer is connected to the right person.
  • Never Leave the Customer on Hold for Too Long: If a hold is necessary, check in regularly and explain the reason for the wait.
  • Never Show a Lack of Knowledge: Be knowledgeable about your products or services and seek help if you are unsure about something.
  • Never Dismiss Customer Complaints: Treat all complaints seriously and investigate them thoroughly.
  • Never Take Criticism Personally: Remain objective and focus on resolving the issue rather than reacting emotionally.
  • Never Use Technical Jargon: Avoid using technical terms that the customer might not understand; use simple and clear language.
  • Never Rush the Customer: Allow the customer to express their concerns fully without making them feel hurried.
  • Never Fail to Follow Up: If a follow-up is promised, ensure it is done in a timely manner to show the customer that their issue is important.

Practical Examples and Alternatives

  • Instead of "I don’t know," say: "Let me find that information for you."
  • Instead of "That’s not my job," say: "Let me connect you with the person who can help you."
  • Instead of "You’re wrong," say: "I understand your perspective. Let’s look at this together."
  • Instead of "Calm down," say: "I can see that this is frustrating. Let’s see how we can resolve it."
  • Instead of "We can’t do that," say: "Here’s what we can do to help."

Types of Orders

  • AFN: (Sold and fulfilled by Amazon)
    • Refund
    • Replacement
    • Repair
    • Discount
  • FBA: (Fulfilled by Amazon, sold by third party seller)
    • Refund only
  • CSBA: (Customer service by Amazon, sold and fulfilled by third party seller)
    • Refund only
    • Contact seller to confirm the return
    • Contact seller in case of delay
    • Contact seller for product troubleshooting
  • MFN: (Market place Fulfillment Network, sold and fulfilled by third party seller)
    • Warranty by the seller
    • Shipping fees set by the seller
    • The only type of order which is not covered by Prime
    • Note: Advice to contact the seller for requests
  • Warehouse Deals: (AFN items but used/missing accessories)
    • Refund
    • Discount
  • Global Store: (Amazon sites)
    • Refund on return only
  • Try Before You Buy: (Amazon Wardrobe)
    • For Prime members only
    • Ship up to 6 items at a time to try on free at home for 7 days
    • Charged only for the items selected to keep

4- MFN: Marketplace Fulfillment Network

Sold: Sold, fulfilled, delivered, and customer service by the seller. Amazon is just a platform.

Actions and Options:

  • Contact seller via email: Customers can reach out for inquiries.
  • Raise an A-Z claim: If the seller does not respond in 48 hours.

Seller has 2 days to respond. The response will appear in the message center, and the customer can reply.

If the seller doesn’t respond in 48 hours, an A-Z claim can be raised. Wait until EDD+3 days pass before raising the claim.

A-Z claims team will take over the case within 7 days.

Max Periods:

  • A-Z claim: 6 months
  • Appeal for rejected claims: 30 days
