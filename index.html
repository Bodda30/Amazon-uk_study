Greeting and Verification Process

Greeting:

  • Thank you for contacting Amazon, this is (Your Name).
  • May I start by having your first and last name, please?

Verification Process:

  • I'm afraid we'll need to verify your account through email or phone number.
  • May I have your email address phonetically or your phone number?
  • I've sent you a link, could you please press on the link in order to access your account?

Phrases for when the caller is not the account holder:

  • I'm afraid this is not the name on the account, could you check your account again, please?
  • Just to confirm, are you the account holder?
  • I'm afraid we won't be able to proceed without verifying your account.
  • We need to contact the account holder in order to proceed.
  • We have to take their permission to continue on their behalf.

1- AFN: Amazon Fulfillment Network

Sold: Fulfilled by Amazon (Prime covered)

Actions and Options:

  • Full refund: If the customer is unsatisfied with the product.
  • Replacement: If the product is defective.
  • Repair: When applicable, such as for electronics.
  • Discounts: For future purchases as a goodwill gesture.

2- FBA: Fulfilled by Amazon

Sold: Sold by seller, fulfilled and delivered by Amazon (Prime covered)

Actions and Options:

  • Full refund: If the product is not as described.

3- CSBA: Customer Service by Amazon

Sold: Fulfilled by seller, customer service by amazon and delivered by seller (Prime covered)

Actions and Options:

  • Full refund: If the product does not meet customer expectations.

4- MFN: Marketplace Fulfillment Network

Sold: Sold, fulfilled, delivered, and customer service by the seller. Amazon is just a platform ( only order not coverd by prime ).

Actions and Options:

  • Contact seller via email: Customers can reach out for inquiries.
  • Raise an A-Z claim: If the seller does not respond in 48 hours.

Seller has 2 days to respond. The response will appear in the message center, and the customer can reply.

If the seller doesn’t respond in 48 hours, an A-Z claim can be raised. Wait until EDD+3 days pass before raising the claim.

A-Z claims team will take over the case within 7 days.

Max Periods:

  • A-Z claim: 6 months
  • Appeal if rejected: 30 days

5- Global Store

  • Don't include Prime
  • Mainland only (Ireland not included)
  • If return shipping fees are more than 80% of the product cost, the customer keeps the product and gets a full refund
  • If the shipping fees are less than 15 pounds and the product is returned, a receipt is not needed and we refund the shipping fees
  • If it's less than 15 pounds but the product is marked as not returned, we request a receipt from the customer before we refund
  • If the customer lost the receipt, we ask them to request another receipt from the carrier

6- Try Before You Buy

  • Available on selected items (Prime only)
  • The customer has seven days to mark the item as "keep" or "return"
  • If the customer marks "keep", the amount gets charged
  • If the customer marks "return", they have 30 days to return the item
  • If the customer does not reply to the email, the amount will be charged after 30 days

7- Stuck Orders

Issues:

  • EDD or tracking is not available
  • EDD is today or has passed
  • EDD has changed 3 or more times

What to Do:

  • Check if the payment has been charged
  • Check if the product is in stock
  • Check if there is a previous ticket raised or not
  • Check if there is a cancel button available or not

If Yes:

  • Help the customer cancel and reorder

If No:

  • Raise a TT (ticket) for the product to be dispatched or canceled

8- WMS: Post Dispatch – Where is My Stuff?

1. Common Issues:

  • Lost
  • Not Delivered
  • Rejected
  • Damaged
  • Delivery Attempt
  • Out for Delivery
  • Delayed
  • Undeliverable
  • DNR (Delivery Not Received)

2. Scenarios and Actions:

If:

  • Lost in transit
  • Delayed
  • Rejected
  • Damaged

Or:

  • Refused, Stopped, or Returned
  • Delivery Attempt
  • Out for Delivery
  • DNR

Then: Advise the customer to wait for the Estimated Delivery Date (EDD) or Final Resolution Date (FRD).

3. Special Cases:

  • High-Value Items: An OTP (One-Time Password) is sent to the customer when the item is marked "out for delivery".
  • 18+ Deliveries: The carrier will request the customer’s ID and signature.

4. Handling Delivery Not Received (DNR):

Before proceeding, ask the following questions to confirm the situation:

  1. Have you checked your safe places, neighbors, or household members to see if they received the item on your behalf?
  2. Could you please confirm your delivery address?
  3. If a delivery photo is available, can you confirm the location shown in the photo is your address/workplace?

5. What to Do:

Check if the customer has reported two previous DNRs within the last 30 days at the same delivery address (Amazon carrier only).

Transfer to SDS:

  • If the item is worth more than £800.
  • If delivered to the wrong address despite specific delivery instructions (Amazon carriers only).
  • If this is the 3rd DNR at the same address with no FRD, pull the Andon Cord.

6. Handling CAP (Customer Abuse Protocol):

If a CAP warning appears, take action if possible (e.g., refund, replacement).

If unable to act, advise waiting for FRD/EDD, and escalate if necessary.

Trainer Notes

Order Journey

Once the CST places the order, an email is sent.

  1. Plan to Ship: CST has 30 mins to:
    • Cancel
    • Reduce quantity
  2. Shipping Soon: Early stage of dispatch:
    • Change address
    • Change payment
  3. Dispatched: A second email is sent to CST with EDD and Tracking number. No changes can be done in this stage.

    Note:

    • After the order is dispatched, some options might be available: re-schedule, re-direct shipment, add delivery instructions, stop and return.
    • You can only re-schedule the order any day after EDD, not before.
    • Re-direct shipment doesn't change the address on the order but sends the update to the carrier.
    • Stop and return requests will result in a refund once the order returns to the return center, though it's not guaranteed.

WMS (Where Is My Stuff)

Pre-Dispatch

Orders stuck before dispatch due to:

  • EDD + Shipping status unknown
  • EDD is today or passed
  • EDD has changed 3 or more times

What to Do?

  • Check if payment is approved
  • Check if the item is out of stock
  • Check annotations
  • If cancel option is available: offer CST to cancel and re-order

Post-Dispatch

If cancel option is unavailable, follow the unable-to-cancel policy and create a TT (ticket) for dispatch or cancellation. SLA: 24-72 hours.

Note: If the order is canceled, the authorization is only on hold, reflecting back to the CST account in:

  • 3-5 business days if OPM
  • 2-24 hours if GC

Handling Phrases:

  • “I’m sorry your order hasn’t been dispatched. I can see the item is out of stock. Our FC is working to restock it and will dispatch it ASAP.”
  • “I see delays in the dispatch process. I’ll contact FC to dispatch it as soon as possible.”
  • “I’ve annotated the order for priority delivery once it’s dispatched.”
  • “I understand where you come from, and I’ll take care of this for you.”

Post-Dispatch Scenarios

  • Lost, undeliverable, damaged, rejected, delayed, delivery attempt, DNR

For DNR (Delivered Not Received)

Check:

  • If CST checked safe places, neighbors, or household members
  • Delivery address confirmation
  • Photo on order for incorrect location

If all checks pass, escalate or transfer to SDS for resolution.

Payment Methods and Gift Cards

Types of Payment Methods:

  1. Premium (Prime):
    • Second day delivery
    • Free shipping
  2. Standard (Regular):
    • 2 to 3 days delivery
    • Shipment fees apply
  3. Rush Delivery:
    • Same day delivery
    • Additional fees, even for Prime members
  4. Amazon Day:
    • Selected fixed date for shipment
    • Available even for Prime CSTs
  5. Wishlist
  6. One-Click

Gift Card Types:

  1. Mechanical Gift Card:
    • Code sent via email
  2. Physical Gift Card:
    • CST can order gift cards and scratch off the gray area for the code
    • If CST has scratched out a few numbers, ask for three consecutive numbers from the middle part of the code
    • If all numbers are scratched out, ask for the serial number, front and back pictures of the gift card, and a photo of the receipt, then escalate to Gift Card team
    • If the CST lost the receipt, ask for the serial number and front and back pictures of the gift card
    • If the CST lost the gift card, ask for the receipt and escalate
  3. Branded Gift Card:
    • If any issue arises, CST should contact the brand directly

Gift Card Redemption:

  • If CST redeems a Gift Card by mistake, unredeeming is possible only if the amount on the Gift Card remains unchanged
  • Gift Cards cannot be used globally
  • Mixed payment methods (bank card & Gift Card) can be used

Refund Process:

  • If payment was made by bank card, refund can be issued to either OPM (Original Payment Method) or Gift Card
  • If payment was made by Gift Card, refund is issued to the Gift Card
  • If mixed payment methods were used:
    • If refund is issued to OPM, the amount paid by bank card will reflect on the bank card, and the amount paid by Gift Card will reflect back on the Gift Card
  • If OPM is selected for a Gift Card payment, the amount will still reflect back to the Gift Card
  • Partial payments can be made with a Gift Card

Compensation for Delayed Orders:

  • If it's a standard order, compensation is issued for shipment fees
  • If it's a Prime order, compensation is issued as 5 Gift Cards or 5 promotional discounts
  • If a Prime CST has experienced multiple delays, offer a free Prime month
  • The 5 promotions are applied automatically on the next order at checkout

Customer Service Tips

Never Do List

  • Never Ignore the Customer: Always acknowledge the customer's presence and their issues promptly.
  • Never Be Rude or Disrespectful: Maintain a polite and respectful tone, regardless of the customer's attitude.
  • Never Interrupt the Customer: Let the customer finish speaking before you respond.
  • Never Blame the Customer: Avoid placing blame on the customer for the problem they are experiencing.
  • Never Make Promises You Can't Keep: Only commit to actions you can deliver on to maintain trust.
  • Never Use Negative Language: Avoid phrases like “you can’t,” “I won’t,” “it’s not possible.” Instead, focus on what you can do.
  • Never Argue with the Customer: Stay calm and professional, even if the customer is angry or unreasonable.
  • Never Give Incomplete Information: Provide clear, complete, and accurate information to the customer.
  • Never Transfer Without Explanation: Always explain why a transfer is necessary and ensure the customer is connected to the right department.
  • Never Forget Follow-ups: If you promise to follow up, ensure you do so to reinforce reliability.

A to Z Information for Success

  • A: Active listening: Always listen to the customer’s concerns without interruption.
  • B: Be patient: Customers may be upset; patience can help calm the situation.
  • C: Clear communication: Speak clearly and avoid jargon that the customer may not understand.
  • D: Document interactions: Keep records of customer interactions for future reference.
  • E: Empathy: Show understanding and compassion towards the customer’s feelings.
  • F: Follow up: Ensure to follow up with customers after resolving their issues.
  • G: Goal-oriented: Aim to achieve a positive outcome for the customer.
  • H: Honesty: Be honest about what you can and cannot do.
  • I: Information: Provide accurate and relevant information promptly.
  • J: Justify actions: Explain why certain actions were taken to the customer.
  • K: Keep calm: Maintain composure, even when faced with difficult customers.
  • L: Listen: Pay attention to both verbal and non-verbal cues.
  • M: Manage stress: Stay calm, even during challenging interactions.
  • N: Non-defensive: Take customer feedback constructively.
  • O: Offer alternatives: If a solution isn’t available, provide alternatives.
  • P: Personalize service: Use the customer's name and personalize interactions.
  • Q: Quick response: Aim to resolve issues in a timely manner.
  • R: Respect: Treat every customer with respect and kindness.
  • S: Stay calm: Keep your composure, even if the customer is upset.
  • T: Tone of voice: Maintain a friendly and approachable tone.
  • U: Understand policies: Familiarize yourself with Amazon’s return and refund policies.
  • V: Validate feelings: Let customers know their feelings are valid.
  • W: Willingness to help: Show enthusiasm in helping the customer.
  • X: eXpectations: Manage customer expectations realistically.
  • Y: You matter: Make customers feel valued and important.
  • Z: Zeal for service: Approach every interaction with passion and dedication.

How to Handle Customers

To effectively handle customers, consider the following tips:

  • Remain calm and composed, even in challenging situations.
  • Use active listening techniques to fully understand customer concerns.
  • Empathize with the customer’s feelings and validate their emotions.
  • Offer clear and concise solutions to their problems.
  • Follow up with customers after resolving their issues to ensure satisfaction.

Empathy and Sympathy Examples

  • Example of Empathy: "I understand how frustrating it must be to receive a damaged item. I'm here to help you resolve this."
  • Example of Sympathy: "I'm so sorry to hear that you've had a bad experience. Let’s work together to make it right."
